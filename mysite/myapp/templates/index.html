Our Mission

The Boston College Machine Learning Group is a hub of highly motivated undergraduate students committed to advancing the field of machine learning.

Our mission is to develop, explore, and apply machine learning techniques to solve real-world problems and to promote knowledge and skills in AI through workshops, guest lectures, and hands-on projects.

I don't know anything about AI or programming. Should I still join?

Yes! The first MIG lecture each year is a crash course in machine learning and following lectures are approachable to a general audience. As the year goes on, we will dive into increasingly complex topics, but we also strive to provide a space to ask any questions no matter how rudimentary. Alongside the resources we share during meetings, our resource page is perfect for a beginner to hone their understanding and comfort with overtime.

Could MIG help me get involved with research?

Yes! The eboard and your fellow club members have connections in the BC research community and beyond. Just ask!

Can I give a talk at MIG?

Anyone in MIG can volunteer to give a talk at our weekly meetings. Talks typically run ~30 minutes and are supplemented with a slide show (and the occasional notebook). All talks are recorded and posted on the Youtube channel. Signing up to give a talk implies consent to be recorded. If you have any questions or concerns, please reach out to an eboard member.
